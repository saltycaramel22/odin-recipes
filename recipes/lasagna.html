Lasagna Recipe

What is lasagna?

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of flat pasta sheets alternated with rich meat or vegetable ragu, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and a generous topping of melted mozzarella and Parmesan. Each layer is baked to perfection, resulting in a hearty, flavorful casserole with a crisp, golden-brown crust and a savory, tender interior. This comforting and versatile dish is a staple in Italian cuisine and beloved worldwide for its deliciously satisfying combination of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

Pasta:

  • Lasagna noodles

Meat Sauce (Ragu):

  • Ground beef (or a mix of ground beef and pork)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Carrot (optional)
  • Celery (optional)
  • Crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Bay leaf

Cheese Mixture:

  • Ricotta cheese
  • Egg
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley or dried parsley (optional)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Additional Cheese:

  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

Béchamel Sauce (optional, often used in place of or alongside ricotta mixture):

  • Butter
  • All-purpose flour
  • Milk
  • Salt
  • Nutmeg

Assembly:

  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery (if using) and sauté until softened.
  4. Add the ground beef (or mix of beef and pork) to the skillet and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  5. Stir in the crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce, tomato paste, red wine (if using), salt, black pepper, sugar (if using), dried oregano, dried basil, and bay leaf. Let the sauce simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley (if using), salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  7. To make the béchamel sauce (if using), melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes. Gradually whisk in the milk and continue to cook until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and nutmeg.
  8. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  9. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  10. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture (or béchamel sauce) over the noodles.
  11. Sprinkle a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese and grated Parmesan cheese over the ricotta mixture.
  12. Repeat the layers (meat sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, and cheese) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  13. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  14. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly with a golden-brown crust.
  15. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving to allow it to set.
  16. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley, if desired, and serve.
